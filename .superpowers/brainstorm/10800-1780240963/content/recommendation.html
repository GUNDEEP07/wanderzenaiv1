Why this works better than pure aurora

Four specific decisions that separate attractive from usable.

01 — CONTRAST
Drama fades as trust builds
The aurora glows on the landing page create the "wow" moment. Once users are in the dashboard or form, those effects are gone — the signal is clarity, not spectacle. Users feel the app is fast and focused.
02 — TOUCH TARGETS
Every tap target is ≥44px
Full-width CTAs on mobile, 52px nav height, 36px stepper buttons on the form. Nothing frustrating to tap. The form screen removes all decoration so the user's thumb never hits the wrong thing.
03 — COGNITIVE LOAD
One action per screen on mobile
The form shows one question at a time with a single "Continue →" CTA. The explore page leads with scrollable tabs — one decision (continent) before showing cards. No visual noise competes with the decision.
04 — CONSISTENCY
Same nav, same padding, same card pattern
Logo left, CTA right, 16px horizontal padding, glass cards with rgba(255,255,255,0.025) background on every content screen. Users learn the pattern once and apply it everywhere. No surprises.
