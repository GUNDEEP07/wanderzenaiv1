The honest breakdown

What each theme actually says to a user who lands on the page.

A — Full Dark
"This is a serious AI tool"
Feels premium and modern. Great for developer tools, productivity apps, AI platforms. But for a travel app targeting everyday users planning holidays — it can feel cold and slightly uninviting.
Best for: AI-native audience, tech users
B — Full Light
"I trust this, it's friendly"
Warm, readable, inviting. Photos pop. Easier to read outdoors on mobile. Feels like a magazine or a travel brand — Airbnb, Lonely Planet energy. The Fraunces font looks exceptional on cream. But loses the "AI product" edge.
Best for: Mainstream travel audience, older users
C — Hybrid ★
"Modern AI that I can trust"
Dark dramatic entry signals "this is AI-powered and premium." Light warm body signals "this is for you, it's readable, it's friendly." The transition between them mirrors the product journey — excitement → clarity → action.
Best for: Everyone. The product you're building.
★ My recommendation
Dark hero + nav. Warm cream body (#f7f4ef). Everywhere.
Keep the aurora dark hero on the landing page — it's your first impression and it works. But below the hero fold, and on every other page (Dashboard, Explore, Pricing, Forms), switch to a warm cream background with dark text. Cards become white with a soft shadow. The teal accent stays exactly the same.

This is how Stripe, Linear, and Lemon Squeezy all work — dark for the dramatic entry, light for the usable product. It converts better because light surfaces are more readable in daylight on mobile, and trust signals (pricing, testimonials) are stronger on white.
